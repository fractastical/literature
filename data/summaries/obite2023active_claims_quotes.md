## Key Claims and Hypotheses

1.  The authors propose an active inference-based algorithm to address the sum rate maximization problem in a UAV-assisted cognitive NOMA network. This algorithm transforms the problem into an abnormality minimization by utilizing a generalized state-space model.
2.  The primary hypothesis is that active inference, rooted in cognitive neuroscience, can provide a more adaptable and resilient framework for resource allocation in dynamic wireless networks compared to traditional optimization approaches.
3.  The authors investigate joint subchannel and power allocation for a Uplin UAV-assisted cognitive NOMA network, aiming to maximize the sum rate.
4.  The core claim is that the proposed Active-GDBN framework learns a generative model of the radio environment, enabling the UAV to continuously adapt its resource allocation policies based on observed environmental changes.
5.  The paper’s central claim is that the proposed Active-GDBN algorithm achieves a higher cumulative sum rate compared to benchmark schemes.
