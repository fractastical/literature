### OverviewThis paper investigates Tactile Active Inference Reinforcement Learning (Tactile-AIRL) for efficient robotic manipulation skill acquisition. The authors propose a novel method that integrates active inference with reinforcement learning, utilizing a vision-based tactile sensor to enhance the perception of the manipulation task. The core idea is to minimize free energy during action, thereby achieving a more precise understanding of the environment and enabling agents to learn skills with minimal sampling and to adapt to sparse rewards. The research demonstrates significant improvements in training efficiency compared to traditional reinforcement learning methods, particularly in non-prehensile object pushing tasks.### MethodologyThe authors introduce Tactile-AIRL, a method that combines active inference with reinforcement learning.
