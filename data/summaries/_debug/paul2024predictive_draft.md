The authors examine two decision-making schemes within active inference – ‘planning’ and ‘learning from experience’ – aiming to understand how agents navigate complex environments. The research focuses on balancing data-complexity trade-offs between these strategies, leveraging the strengths of both to facilitate balanced decision-making.
