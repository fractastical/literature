## Key Claims and Hypotheses

1.  The authors propose that living organisms construct generative models to navigate the world adaptively, rather than solely to understand it.
2.  Generative models are crucial for enabling action-perception loops, where predictions guide actions and feedback refines those predictions.
3.  The brain employs a hierarchical predictive coding framework, where higher-level areas generate predictions that are compared to sensory input at lower levels.
4.  The minimization of variational free energy (or prediction error) is a fundamental principle governing brain function, driving adaptive behavior.
5.  The brain learns to represent the world through a combination of explicit and implicit models, encompassing both external entities and internal states.
6.  The brain’s generative models are not static but evolve through learning, adapting to the organism’s ecological niche.
7.  The brain’s generative models are characterized by their ability to generate counterfactual predictions, enabling planning and imagining.
