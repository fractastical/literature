## Key Claims and Hypotheses

1. **The Active Inference Framework as a Model for Neurofeedback:** The authors propose Active Inference as a general framework for understanding and modeling neurofeedback training. This framework posits that the subject’s brain is actively constructing a model of its environment and that neurofeedback can be used to refine this model.

2. **Importance of Subject’s Internal Model:** The authors highlight the significance of the subject’s internal model in neurofeedback. They argue that the effectiveness of neurofeedback depends on the ability of the subject to accurately represent and control their environment.

3. **Role of Feedback in Model Refinement:** The paper suggests that neurofeedback works by providing feedback that allows the subject to refine their internal model of the environment. This feedback is used to adjust the subject’s actions and ultimately achieve a desired state.

4. **Hypothesis: Subject’s Actions Drive Learning:** The authors hypothesize that learning in neurofeedback is driven by the subject’s actions and their ability to adjust their behavior based on feedback.

5. **Hypothesis: Model-Based Learning:** The authors propose that neurofeedback training is fundamentally a model-based learning process, where the subject builds and refines a model of their environment.

6. **Importance of Accurate Feedback:** The authors emphasize that the quality and accuracy of the feedback provided during neurofeedback training are crucial for successful learning.
