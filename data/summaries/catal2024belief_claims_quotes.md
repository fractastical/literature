1.  **Main Claim:** Naively sharing posterior beliefs between agents in an active inference framework leads to undesirable social dynamics, specifically the formation of echo chambers and self-doubt, hindering collaborative performance.

2.  **Hypothesis:** Sharing likelihood information rather than full posterior beliefs will mitigate these negative social dynamics and improve collaborative performance.

3.  **Key Claim:** The paper demonstrates that agents, when sharing full posterior beliefs, reinforce each other’s biases, leading to echo chambers.

4.  **Key Claim:** Agents’ confidence in their beliefs is undermined when they share information, leading to self-doubt and a rejection of observed evidence.

5.  **Key Claim:** The core of the paper is to explore the dynamics of belief sharing in multi-agent systems, specifically focusing on the detrimental effects of sharing full posterior beliefs.
