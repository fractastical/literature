## Key Claims and Hypotheses

1.  **Central Claim:** The paper proposes a new decision-making scheme within the framework of active inference, combining ‘planning’ and ‘learning from experience’ to address the challenges posed by unexpected observations.

2.  **Hypothesis:** Integrating planning and experience-based learning will improve the robustness and adaptability of agents operating in complex environments, particularly those with high data complexity.

3.  **Claim:** Active inference offers a biologically plausible approach to intelligent behavior by focusing on agents accumulating and maximizing evidence during their lifetime, enabling perception, learning, and decision-making.

4.  **Hypothesis:** A mixed model combining planning and counterfactual learning will lead to more efficient and robust decision-making compared to relying solely on one approach.

5.  **Claim:** The variational free energy minimization provides a universal framework to model discrete state-space environments, where agents learn to optimize their decisions by minimizing expected free energy.
