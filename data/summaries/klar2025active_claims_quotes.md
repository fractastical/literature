## Key Claims and Hypotheses

1.  **Main Claim:** The paper proposes a novel Active Inference (AIF) model for mouse pointing and clicking behavior, demonstrating that it can generate plausible pointing trajectories and clicks, similar to human performance.

2.  **Hypothesis:**  A continuous AIF model, incorporating perceptual delay compensation and preference distributions over observations, can effectively model human pointing behavior.

3.  **Key Finding:** The AIF agent produces trajectories and click events that closely resemble human pointing behavior, particularly when considering the impact of perceptual delay.

4.  **Key Finding:** The AIF model does not require manual parameter tuning for each target, unlike traditional optimal control-based approaches.

5.  **Key Finding:** The AIF model’s ability to generate realistic pointing behavior is largely due to its ability to predict the cursor’s position based on the observed environment, rather than relying on explicit control commands.
