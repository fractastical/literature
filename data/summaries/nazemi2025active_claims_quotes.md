## Key Claims and Hypotheses

1.  **Core Claim:** Active Inference (AIF) offers a novel framework for energy control and planning in smart buildings and communities, addressing limitations of traditional optimization and reinforcement learning approaches.

2.  **Hypothesis:** The dual-layer AIF architecture – a continuous agent for building-level control and a discrete agent for community-level management – can effectively handle the inherent complexities of building energy systems, including non-stationary dynamics, partial observability, and uncertainties.

3.  **Claim:** AIF’s reliance on Bayesian principles, generative modeling, and free energy minimization enables it to seamlessly integrate prior knowledge, real-time observations, and adaptive control strategies.

4.  **Hypothesis:** The integration of AIF with day-ahead planning and market interactions will improve energy efficiency and reduce reliance on expensive energy sources.

5.  **Claim:** The proposed AIF approach is the first to demonstrate a distributed AIF architecture, highlighting new opportunities for privacy-preserving and uncertainty-aware control strategies in engineering applications.
