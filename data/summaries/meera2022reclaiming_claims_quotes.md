## Key Claims and Hypotheses

1.  The authors propose a new account of attention based on precision-modulated action and perception, distinguishing it from traditional saliency models that rely on static saliency maps.
2.  The core hypothesis is that attention arises from the active inference of underlying causes of sensory data, driven by the need to minimize uncertainty about the causes of observed data.
3.  The authors posit that precision control, i.e., the ability to accurately estimate the causes of sensory data, is a fundamental mechanism underlying both perception and action.
4.  The research investigates the role of rhythmic precision modulation in coordinating perception and action, suggesting that this rhythmicity is crucial for efficient information processing.
5.  The paper argues that the precision-modulated account can be effectively implemented for robotics, facilitating state estimation, system identification, and action selection.
