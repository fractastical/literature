## Key Claims and Hypotheses

1. **Central Claim:** The paper proposes a new approach to understanding agency by interpreting systems as solutions to Partially Observable Markov Decision Processes (POMDPs). This means a system can be considered an agent if it can be represented as solving a POMDP.

2. **Hypothesis:** The authors hypothesize that existing frameworks for understanding agency (e.g., Dennett’s stances) are incomplete and fail to capture the full complexity of how systems can exhibit intelligent behavior.

3. **Claim:** A system’s ability to be interpreted as an agent depends on its capacity to parameterize beliefs about a hidden state and act rationally with respect to those beliefs, as defined by a POMDP.

4. **Claim:** The core idea is that the dynamics of a system (its internal state and how it evolves) can be modeled and interpreted in terms of a POMDP, allowing us to understand how the system makes decisions.

5. **Hypothesis:** The paper suggests that the framework of POMDPs provides a more formal and rigorous way to characterize agency than existing approaches.
